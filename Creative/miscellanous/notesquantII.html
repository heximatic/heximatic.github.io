Reliability

Consistency - If you keep firing at the bullseye, how often do you hit the same spot? Ranges from 0-1 Types of Reliability Test-Retest reliability

        - Take test at time 1, wait, test at time 2, correlate scores
        - Strength: controls for type of error related to time
    **Alternate Forms**
        - Administer test one, administer second test after a period of time that is as similar as possible to the original, correlated test 1 with test 2
    **Internal Consistency**
        - Give people series of items at one time point
        - How consistently do they respond?
        - Strength: easy to calculate and collect data
        - Weakness: can't pick up variance due to time problems
     **Split-half reliability**
        - Instead of creating alternate forms, split test in half and correlate with itsself
        - best way to do: odd-even split

Validity

How do we determine validity? Criterion-related validity:

  • does it correlate with meaningful non-test criteria?
    • Ex. if interviewing for a job, people that score high on a test should have similar test scores in the future
  • Predictive
    • Happening before the job
  • Concurrent
    • Not going to have much variation; surveying people who are all fairly similar

Construct Does the test correlate with other tests the way the theory would expect? Nomological Scale

    - Pick constructs that should correlate positively with other scales (ex. our grit questions and Duckworth's scale)

Analysis of Variance

If analyzing multiple variables, this is most useful. Reduces the error rate that T-test gives.

T-Tests (comparing two groups)

Single sample

  • Is the sample mean different from the population mean? (sample -> population) Independent Sample
  • To test whether two independent sample means come from different populations (sample <-> sample, no common factors) Paired groups
  • Analyzing between two related groups that are significantly different (sample <-> sample, one or more common factors)

    What do we use and when?

    If there are two groups being compared,
  • Independent groups T-Test If there are more than two groups,
  • One-Way ANOVA If there are Multiple factors (and potentially more than two groups being compared),
  • Two-Way ANOVA
