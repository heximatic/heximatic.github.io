Blood And Stone (ch 1, part 1)

Before him lie swirling, shivering violets and mysterious particles. It had such a mysterious and vibrant glow; colors thematically opposite to the fire and brimstone with which the portal originated, but fitting nonetheless. There were few things in the world like it- in fact, the only thing he could think to compare it to was the eyes and physique of an Enderman, a lanky and thin creature he had only seen in books. It was just as novel as it was frightening, but more than anything, it was interesting. His curiosity would get the better of him this day, and this would set the course for the rest of his life.

Steve was a temporary resident of Cobblebrook Village, a quaint little place nestled in the belly of the Lapis valley. He lived on the outskirts of town for its peace and quiet and closeness to the forest, but he’d spend much of his day in town anyways. Odd jobs were abundant here, and so he nearly always had something he could be helping with. The only reason he didn’t have a steady job was because his aspiration was not to settle down. He only planned to be here for a couple of years and then move on, but until then he wanted to see what this town was about.

He’d heard rumors that this place had some information on some archeological mysteries, but thus far, the place hadn’t quite lived up to that. He’d found some interesting books in Cobblebrook library, but they were more like the ramblings of an individual who spent a little too much time caving and came out all wrong than someone trustworthy and competent. Anything worth discovering was already in museums in the towns over, and even they weren’t that interesting. This portal, however, entirely changed his view of the place.

It was quite out of the way of any path, and the only reason he’d come across it was on a search for wildberries. It was buried in dirt and mud on a cliffside, and anyone less keen in the senses was likely to miss it. Digging it up took quite some time, with it being about half a day’s walk and in quite a hidden spot. Between his jobs and tending to his small farm, it took him a couple of days to even gather what he needed. His patience would reward him in the form of a couple of discoveries, the first of which being Blood-stone, as he named it.

It was an organic, stony substance, surrounding the base of the portal. It was warm too, like it had never soaked up the cold from the earth around it. This was interesting because it must have been buried there for hundreds of years, the way the trees and plants had wormed their roots through it, but there was no corrosion nor sign that it had been damaged. This undoubtedly piqued his interest.

Over a few days, he conducted several experiments on it. He sliced off a sample and dunked it into a bucket of water. The water got soaked up like a sponge and evaporated within minutes. And in the process of breaking it, he found some spores. They were dead, but they were unlike anything he’d ever seen- they were small and round and crimson in color, sort of shaped like a spider’s eye with a crimson tail at the end. Among his findings, he noted that the sample wasn’t affected by fire. It didn’t shrink in size, nor did it char when exposed to open flame. Given the time it would burn forever, he deduced. He took note of all of these observations in his notebook, and he continued.

Things began to get strange after these discoveries. The next day, he went to conduct more experiments on the portal, only to find some of the frame had been knocked to the ground. An entire chunk of obsidian was misplaced, which was strange- he was sure that nothing in this forest was strong enough to cause that kind of damage, especially given how sturdy the portal had been thus far. He spent all day attempting to put the frame back together, but when the next day rolled around, even more of the frame had been knocked over. With some investigation, he came across the writing ‘STOP’ messily etched into the dirt nearby. Well, if that wasn’t foreboding. He was far too curious to stop now though.
        It was raining by the next day, so he decided to just stay at home. And by the following day, he was back at the site… and was greeted by a most awful sight. His portal was half buried in this blood stone! He spent another couple of days unburying it without any notable interference. By the twenty-second day, all was repaired again, and he could resume his experiments.

This time, he decided to play a bit more with the fire. A spark of flame flew over to the portal's frame, and in the corner of his eye, he saw a flicker of purple light. And, like that, with another strike of flint and steel, his portal was lit.

 The first thing to enter the portal was his hands on account of them being in the frame. He pulled them away immediately, but noticed no ill effects besides an unnatural warmth. And so he tried it again, deciding this time to use his journal as a test subject. Almost as soon as he did, he felt something grab it and snatch it out of his hand, causing him to gasp and jump back. He swore he could see the faint outline of a humanoid through the portal’s frame. Now that he had the motivation of going to retrieve his journal, that and his curiosity far outweighed any fear he felt in that moment. He took a breath, stepped forward, and poked his head inside.

The Man with White Eyes (in a sea of red) (ch 1, pt 2)

Once his head was through the portal, a wave of nausea hit him. Thick and muggy heat hit his face, and it filled his lungs, making his breath heavier. He coughed and hacked as his body adjusted to the stifling air. The heat made his eyes sting, but he kept them open to see what this unfamiliar place held.

He was greeted with plateaus, valleys, floating islands of the same substance that he’d seen around the portal- blood stone. In the distance, there was a group of bipedal figures wandering aimlessly. He concluded that the dimension, while intense on his body, was visually quite boring. There wasn’t any reason why his progress should have been destroyed multiple times… unless the miasma was a front.

 As he took a step forward, he felt a different texture beneath his shoes. It wasn’t blood stone. It was… grass? Maybe moss, all glowing and blue. It was so different from everything else around it. The blue would certainly be noticed for miles in a place like this; so where was it coming from?

 He took a couple more steps, watching the grass appear below his feet each time he made contact. It was so mesmerizing that he didn’t notice the portal ahead of him. Overworld air filled his lungs- he automatically sucked in deep breaths to replace the stiff air of fire and blood stone. He just as quickly processed that he was not where he was trying to be.  A feeling of dread welled up in him. He cautiously turned his head towards it and reached for the dagger in his back pocket.

His portal was behind him. And with his portal, a cloaked man. It was hard to make out through the fog, but their cloak flowed over their form, making them look large and imposing. They had their arms outstretched towards the portal. With a concentrated stare, they were making it disappear.

Steve stared at them, frightened and bewildered.

Once the portal was gone, they finally looked over at Steve. Their face was contorted with disdain, and eyes glowing white. They were wide too, that sort of frustrated stare that told him he’d be in danger if he kept persisting. And yet…

Before he could even think to start towards it, the being disappeared in a puff of smoke. There was no trace of them besides their footprints.

“Wha- Hey!” He ran to the portal frame, and struggled to light it with sweaty hands. He lit it though, and ran right through, his own burning curiosity motivating him more than any regards for safety. He jumped through. In the wave of nausea, he caught his foot, tripping and falling right into the figure’s trap; a two block deep hole. He hit the ground hard enough to feel it in his bones, and grasped at the netherrack to climb his way out. His position guided him to look up, and he noticed the same figure glaring down at him. They were not happy.

His eyes went wide and he pressed his back against the fleshy netherrack.

“Who are you??”
        They glared at them in silence as he glided down towards him. As he moved, he outstretched a hand. Some sparkling, teal energy flared up in it. Steve could now recognize that he might have just gone too far.
        “I’ll go away, just let me get out of this hole, dude!” His voice was shrill with fear.
        He glimpsed a smirk on the man’s… being’s face. But then it changed. The man had a moment of hesitation, staring down at Steve’s frightened visage. His eyes, once on Steve’s, trailed to his own hand. Something was very wrong.

He jerked his hand away from Steve, and floated away until his powers failed him and he fell to the ground. His cloak, the flowing, silky thing it was, faded into nothingness. It, and his other darkened clothing, replaced by a plain, teal t-shirt and dark blue pants. With this change in attire, Steve could see black veins underneath his skin. They glowed and pulsed as if they were struggling to retain their magic. The man had magic coursing through his veins. He noticed it too, and was quick to hide his arms from the stranger.

He hissed, snapping his head towards Steve.
        “What, what the… I-I didn’t do anything! I swear!”

His focus was on the dagger he was using to climb and make a getaway. As he got his torso over the trap, he jumped to his feet and reached out to grab him. Steve rolled away from him and got to his feet before he could reach him. Steve pointed the dagger at him. He wasn’t there.

Steve scanned in front of himself, uncertain of his location,  until he felt his heat at his back. Before he could turn around, he grabbed his right arm. Steve was quick to switch the blade to his other hand and attempted to stab him. He took Steve’s wrist with his left. Steve strained and struggled, kicking his leg back to hit his knee, but he was gone again.

He was in front of him now, and Steve could see a dangerous look in his white eyes. He was very intentional with his glare. He grabbed Steve by his collar and lifted him up.

“Hear me loud and clear, human. Go. Away.”

That was the first time Steve heard his voice, and most likely the last as was thrown into the portal. He landed on the ground, back in the overworld. He marched through after him, arms out at his sides and palms open. He clenched them into fists and the portal shattered. The veins in his arms pulsed with a white glow. His eyes mirrored the effect.

“Stay AWAY.” His voice boomed like thunder whipping through the sky.

After his warning, he disappeared in a puff of smoke. Steve was left alone, and bewildered, on the cold dirt. There was no greater motivation to stay away than a threat on his life, so he decided to heed his advice and finally leave the portal alone.

Home (ch 1, pt 3)


        It had been decades since someone tried to enter a portal. It was troublesome for him that the remnants of his banishment still remained in some parts of the world, but it wasn’t so hard to ward people off. He just made himself big and scary, threatened a few lives without seriously injuring anyone, and he’d be left alone. And that was routine. It was a little fun scaring them, really. It was the only real entertainment he got besides watching Nether mobs fight each other.

Decades were a while ago. This new human was different. He had done something to him and his domain. He knew what this place was supposed to feel like. He came to know this place like the back of his hands; any changes in the temperature, and the scalding winds from the lava seas below mixing with the ambient air above creating a sort of weather system- he knew it all. The air was cooler than it was meant to be, and all seemed to be brighter. It was like it was making itself more suitable to human invaders…

When he tried to teleport back to his home, he felt a barrier, like the world itself was fighting him and putting him into his place. His eyes grew wide with a mix of fear and frustration as he tried a couple more times. He had become the master of this domain, how dare it defy him?! His frustration became anger, anger that simmered as he, seeing no other choice, resolved to walk.

With every step he took, the terrain below him grew strange and warped. Some parts remained the familiar Netherrack, but there was also the blue grass, and a crimson-colored version of that grass. Finally, there were also basalt spikes which he had to carefully traverse, lest he be impaled.

Another thing he noticed was that, every sixteen blocks, the air seemed to grow a little more unstable. It wasn’t noticeable the first hundred blocks, but after a while it grew to bother him. It was strange and crunchy, like the air on a frigid winter night which was certainly unusual in a place of hellfire. After a sharp turn around a steep incline, he saw something awful.

A wall of netherrack.

The human making an entrance into the world corrupted it. Completely. His castle would be just about a hundred blocks forward but, by this wall, at least the grand stairwell up to the castle would be broken. This prediction was true. The once beautiful netherrack brick staircase, handmade by Herobrine himself over the hundreds of years of his banishment; it was covered in this fresh new nether.

Once he finally dug past the netherrack wall, there was nothing. Well, not exactly. The beautiful netherrack castle was nowhere to be seen, but what was in its place was more fucked up than the wall.

The terrain was completely corrupted.

There were parts that were somehow floating in the air- other parts of the world were completely gone, leading down into an infinite, glowing void. That crunchy air was strongest here- it made his vision blurry, and his peripherals grew dark in a vignette. The world was fighting against its own existence, trying and failing to pave itself anew. Herobrine’s presence was preventing a clean conversion. And that terrified him.

Steve, whoever that man was, changed his world for good. The hundreds of years he spent on his beautiful castle, forging every brick and laying it to perfection- it was gone. Everything he had collected, studied for, every hall and turn he came to know intimately… Herobrine felt grief and hatred filling his lungs. He let out a scream of frustration, and collapsed to the broken ground, digging his nails into the netherrack.

Steve’s face filled his mind and he grit his teeth. His eyes grew bright like flames- burning and threatening to swallow the world in their heat.

“STUPID FUCKING HUM-” he began, then he felt his fingers brushing up against something coarse and rough- brick-like. One of his bricks. He slowly turned his teary eyes towards the object, noting that it was his own work. So where was his castle?

He picked up the brick, wiped his eyes, and scanned the area for anything of the like. There were remnants of his castle. Its magnificence was certainly gone, but its remnants remained. Desperately, he started to scout the area. The more he looked, the more of his masonry turned up. He got close to the strange glow of the void as he continued to look, and finally came across a section of a corridor that was deep underneath.

Maybe his research room was safe, he hoped. He closed his eyes to recite his mental map of the place. Stairs lead up to a lobby. The lobby was about twenty width and twelve length. To the front was a long corridor leading to overworld portals. The left was storage, forge and armory, and right would be where he had a bedroom (though, now he questioned why he had it in the first place if he rarely used it), and after the bedroom was his research, hidden deep underground. He flew about and backtracked to the lobby, and from there he followed his mental map.

He found that his lab was somewhat intact. Beyond the small arch, the quartz room was split in half. His most important work was gone now, the cure, but he still had the materials he needed. At this point, he was glad with what he could get. Someday he’d get the damned recipe to work.

After packing his materials into his inventory, he set off towards the storage. He found that it was also demolished, save for a couple of chests and some things on his racks. He picked up a worn cloak and a scythe. He’d have to set out for a new location in the overworld after finding out where Steve lived. At least he would have if he hadn’t felt an even stranger energy coming from the direction of the portals.

He floated in the direction. There was something new that came from the corruption. A shrine, or summoning ring of some sort. There was a small pool of lava in the center, and four quartz pillars of lava with wither skeleton skulls on top of each of them. Eight blocks of netherrack were on fire, and finally, there was soul sand. Strange particles that hovered above the lava, though they seemed to be docile.

He approached the summoning ring carefully. When he was about thirty blocks away from it, he felt a sharp headache. A strange whispering invaded his ears. It wasn’t coherent, but it wasn’t positive either. These voices were excited, in a bad way. He saw the light in his veins flicker when he approached 20 blocks, and the whispers became a little more coherent.

‘He will return.’

‘This world will be saved.’

‘We will be cleansed of sin.’

‘Praise him!’

‘Praise him!’

‘Praise him!’

‘The Wither!’

“The Wither…” Herobrine hissed. He grit his teeth and tried to get closer (in an attempt to knock down the shrine), only to feel the headache and whispering get stronger. He tripped and fell. He could almost feel the eyes of the voices on him as they spoke.

‘Oh? Who is this?’

‘I believe his name is ‘Herobrine’.’

‘Yes, yes, and he is quite the foolish one.’

‘What is it you mean?’

‘Our Lord does not grant good things to those who act with greed.’

‘Nor wrath. I believe it was wrath that drove him to it?’

‘Yes. The foolish, mad one named Herobrine attempted to inject himself with our lord's power, only to be poisoned!’

A low growl pushed its way up his throat. The voices continued to taunt him.

‘Oh! He will work to cleanse the likes of you too, fool!’

‘He will cleanse everyone in hi-.’

Herobrine teleported away before it could finish speaking. He was more bewildered than upset now. Something needed to be done about this shrine. He couldn’t get to it, but perhaps the human would be impervious to its effects. Plus he could just throw him into the void afterward.

His target?

He teleported into the overworld, near a village. On the outskirts of the village he saw a small cabin. And on that cabin’s porch, he noticed a lonely man rocking in his rocking chair, watching the setting sun. Yes. He would be his target.

The Man with White Eyes? (ch 1, pt 4)

“Do you know anything about a man with white eyes?”

Steve promised he would help a neighbor, by the name of Mark, organize his barn a bit. Specifically his crates, which were all over the place.

Mark put down his crate with a grunt and looked over at Steve. “Ah heard uh a man… long time ago… name starts with an H. That’s bout’ all ah know. Ask the librarian?”

“I’ve been in the library a couple of times… don’t think I've seen anything of that sort.” Steve huffed as he lifted another box.

“You’d be surprised what ya find in there. I went there with ma’ daughter one day and found a book about there being another world. And I thought, ‘in the sky?’ Apparently it's a world underneath us, with many pig mans and giant headed ghouls. It sounded fake to me but what do I know?” Mark laughed.

That piqued Steve’s interest. “Oh? What was it called?”

“Um… the… whatsit… Nethar? Nether? Might be it.”

“What’d the cover look like?”

“It was all spooky and dark and stuff… lots of red.”

“Hmmm…” Steve was deep in thought as he stacked up another crate. Was that the place he was in?

“Hm? What’s on your mind, fella?” Mark inquired.

Steve let out a sigh and gave an awkward grin. “Oh, it’s nothing. Just somewhere I thought I might have seen in a dream.”
        “Really? That’s mighty strange… sure it wadn’t a nightmare?”

Steve raised his eyebrows a little. “It could have been.” Maybe that whole experience was just a weird dream. Or a nightmare. As much as he wanted to convince himself that, the bruising on his wrist was more than enough proof that something happened. “I think I'll go check out the library to see if I can find anything on a guy with white eyes after this.”

“Alright. Wish ya luck on that, laddie.”

After thirty more minutes of helping Mark with the boxes (and a healthy payment), Steve set off to the library. He had an hour before his next work, and he’d get his studying started while he could.

The Cobblebrook library was a quaint little place- given that it was the biggest library in town (the first one burned down and they salvaged what they could), it had a sizable amount of information for him. Steve entered the glass double doors and made his way to the front desk. “Do you have any information on a man with white eyes?”

The lady behind the desk, Alex, answered in a jovial tone. “A man with white eyes? Let me see…”
        She stood and started, facing towards the fiction section. Her green eyes widened with realization. “Like… Herobrine?”

“Is that his name?”

Steve had never seen someone walk so fast. He tried his best to follow her pace towards the history section as she sorted through some titles. “Oh… oh my. There aren’t a lot of books here but… I've read about him before…”

“What do you know about him?”

“Well, uh, a good hundred years ago he committed a massacre in Opal, y’know, the city.”

“Oh deer. For real? In real life?” Steve went a little pale.

“Y-yes. 31 people were killed, and 152 injured when he, um, exploded. Into a fireball. Like the explosion of a hundred creepers at once.”

“I always thought that was a myth… that what actually happened was a group of pillagers.”

“Not according to an account in the book ‘Addark’s Greatest Mysteries.’ I don’t remember who the account was by and I know… the title doesn't sound all that trustworthy but…”

Steve wouldn’t trust it if he hadn’t seen the man himself. “I’m still interested. Do you have that book?”

“Yeah… if you want to look at it.”

She went to the biography section of the non-fiction area, eventually locating the book. The cover was sort of falling apart- it was a map that was glued onto the cover of a journal, with the words ‘Addark’s Greatest Mysteries’ in ink. Addark wasn’t very good at graphic design, were they?

He opened up to the table of contents and took note. “The Ender Dragon, the Sea Monster… L-lick?” Steve stifled a laugh. Alex just shrugged.

Steve turned back to the table and his eyes widened at one of the entries. “Steve???”

“What?” Alex looked over his shoulder and her eyebrows raised with interest.

Steve flipped to the content with his name in it to find someone that looked very scarily like himself with Herobrine’s clothing on. Oddly, there wasn’t much to this entry besides a couple of photos, taken in times throughout history. None of these people were related, but the moment one of these people died, another person that looked and acted the near exact same as the older one would be born. Like cosmic historical twins.

“Ummm… okay…” Steve, thoroughly weirded out, flipped back to the first page. He tracked to the page where the information on Herobrine began.

Herobrine, formerly known as Holden Booker, is an interdimensional entity known to sometimes appear in the event that someone attempts to make a portal to the Nether. I will not be detailing how to make that portal here. If one does make a portal and attempts to enter, he will likely appear and kick you, and your party, out within five minutes or so. That is if he hasn’t already successfully thwarted the portal’s structure.

‘Alright. That sounds about right. That’s legit,’ Steve thought.

“Is that what you were looking for?” Alex asked, noting his concentrated stare. Steve grinned and nodded.

“This is perfect. Thank you.”

Steve checked the time on a nearby clock. He had about thirty minutes- he’d read for half that time and get going. Once he was settled comfortably, he started to read.

Details on his origins are quite vague, but he is said to come from Sividia, which was a part of the Galean Federation as of September 1830.

‘Oh my… Sividian…?.’

At some point during the first war, he injected himself with a mysterious fluid.

‘Ew.’

This substance caused him to go haywire. For a couple of days, he lit the South Vitaran trenches, Sividia’s enemy, up in hell. Reports of his anguished screams are detailed in this snippet.

Steve checked out the snippet; it was an incomprehensible script.

He was locked away in a Vitaran prison for about a month until he escaped and created more terror for a couple of days- then he’d crash again. This repeated over and over again. It took a couple of months before his lab was discovered, where a portal was located. There were notes on the portal’s creation, what a ‘little birdy’ told him. What was inside the portal was The Nether, as was written on those notes. The Nether was a hellish place of red, squishy stone and awful, flying, crying heads. Holden had an independent lab where he was creating ‘Superhuman Potions’ to aid him, and his comrades in the war. It did not go as planned.

With this knowledge of the Nether and the Nether portal, many more portals were created. There was a big boom in exploration , until a Galean leader, Saval, suggested that Holden be locked away there. This suggestion was approved, and a prison was made for him there. He only appears here every once and a while, and is generally said to be docile.

Steve looked up at the clock again and noticed that it was time for him to go. He walked up to the front desk, checked out the book, and carried it with him to his next job. There was some information in this book that would certainly come to be useful.

His next job was setting up target practice dummies for an archery range. It wasn’t very hard to do, and it gave him ample time to dwell on Herobrine and if those tales were really true. They sounded pretty genuine. As he set up the posts, he wondered if he’d ever see him again. It was good if he didn’t, of course, but he could never be sure.

He also thought some about the archery range in general. It reminded him of his time in guard training. He remembered helping to build the wall around their village. Laying those bricks down in promise of a brighter future… he’d say things were indeed brighter. They were generally friendly with the neighboring villages and towns.

Steve worked for about two hours- he helped repaint some of the fencing around the archery range, and then went out to buy some more paint because they ran out, and then helped with that. He was surely tired after it all, and enjoyed great, complimentary apple juice, along with his payment.

He went back home afterwards to have lunch, and then left once more to go back to the library. He had some questions about Herobrine, and surely Alex would know. The library was closed during Alex’s lunch break, so Steve sat on the stairwell and continued to read about him.

The massacre of Opal: In 1860, Too many people were rediscovering how to get into the Nether, and, presumably, this roused Herobrine once more. He entered through one of the nearest portals to him, into the town of Opal, and began destroying things with a long scythe. When confronted by authority, he sliced off Officer Terrance Greene’s head. Officer Jonn Wallay managed to slice him across his eye, but he was also quickly decapitated. In Herobrine’s fury, he did something never seen before; he exploded. He had the force of a hundred creepers, blasting a huge crater into South Ruby street. The force collapsed buildings and destroyed homes, killing 25 and injuring an estimated 150.

There were some images of the aftermath.

‘So that did happen… ouch.’

Steve decided he’d had enough about Herobrine, and looked at some other anomalies. Besides the things he’d seen; Entity 303, Null and Mothman were individuals that were identified. There were also locations, like the Nether Fortresses, observed to not be built by Herobrine himself- it was much more of an ancient thing. And deep underground, in their world, there were rumored ‘End Portals’, leading to a dimension called the End. Supposedly.

Alex was back by the time he was done reading.

“Do you need help with anything?”

“Mmm… hmm. Have you seen Herobrine before?”

“What?”

“Y-yeah! Yeah. I saw- I think it was him- I believe.”

Alex gave him a weird look. “Is this why you’re so suddenly interested, hm?”

Steve sighed. “Yeah. I found a portal half buried in the ground and spent an unbelievable amount of time unearthing it- i’ve been out of the village a lot lately because of it- but yeah… I chased him around a bit and he put me in a hole, we fought but… I'm alive. Miraculously, after all that i’ve read.”

“Yeah! That’s quite miraculous. That could have ended terribly…?”

There was a hint in her voice that she didn’t quite believe him, so he showed her where Herobrine had grabbed his arms.

“Oh. I see.”

Steve nodded. Alex decided Steve really needed to take a break from mob fighting, he surely hurt his head in all of it.

He was back home after a couple of hours. He ate his dinner and sat out on his porch afterwards, taking in the cooling air and rocking in his chair. It was peaceful. He felt he could fall asleep right there. Eventually, he stopped rocking and just leaned back, taking in the chirping cicadas and the rustling leaves… and footsteps?

His blue eyes turned in the direction and he picked up his sword from beside him. “Hello? Someone there?”

The walking stopped.

As soon as it stopped, he witnessed its culprit. Herobrine stood before him in a ragged cloak, brandishing a scythe.

The color drained from Steve’s face and he whipped his sword towards Herobrine, who blocked the attack with his own weapon. They clashed loudly.

“Herobrine?!”

“...Where did you learn my name?” There was great annoyance in his voice as he tilted his head. He bore his eyes into Steve’s. “You’re coming with me.”

The Nether (ch 2, pt 1) ( ! graphic depictions of violence)

Steve coughed as his lungs fought the nether air.

“I thought- you didn’t want-me in here!” He fought to be released from Herobrine’s grasp.

“I need something from you,” Herobrine said simply. He released him. Steve didn’t have anywhere to go without materials, and he was at a disadvantage in a place like this. Steve sucked in some deep breaths and tried to adjust to the Nether.

“Follow me. It will not benefit you to disobey me.”

Steve was taken aback by the formality of his speech, but he didn’t trust Herobrine any more.

“Why should I do-”

Herobrine turned to him with foreboding and threatening eyes. Steve just narrowed his eyes on him.

“Why?”

Herobrine groaned, throwing his head back and burying his face in his hands. Nothing else would be able to do what he needed to though, with efficiency anyways.

Steve was glad to annoy him. Whatever Herobrine wanted, he needed Steve alive for it. So he’d taunt him. He brandished a smirk and held his chin in his hands. “Am I making a fuss~?” He gave a fake pout for extra effect.

Herobrine scoffed and rolled his eyes. Without pupils, it was hard to tell.

“You want to know why I brought you here? Your existence in this world changed it. In the place of what used to be my castle is now a summoning ring of sorts. I want you to destroy it.”

“Oh? Why can’t you do it yourself? Wait, you have a castle??”

“Had a castle. And no, I cannot. My body will not allow me to get close to it.”

“Hmm… and what if the same thing happens to me?”

Herobrine stared at him in silence. Steve groaned.

“Fine. I guess it would be interesting… Show me this great sum-.”

Herobrine had begun walking. Steve followed begrudgingly, observing his peripherals for any means of escape. The wall of netherrack caught his attention.

“What’s that about?”

“Corruption.”

Herobrine did not.

Steve watched in awe as they passed through the netherrack wall and was even more tripped out by the glitchy terrain. He had to pause and take it all in.

“Woah…”
